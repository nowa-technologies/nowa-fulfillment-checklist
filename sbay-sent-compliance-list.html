Purpose of this document

This is a single, consolidated list of the US-market compliance requirements for the AIKO smart toy, merged from HQTS's compliance testing list and Tina's list. It is shared with SBay as one reference point so the requirements, their legal basis, risk level and lead time are clear in one place.

The items marked ✅ Committed are the ones NOWA currently intends to complete — but NOWA is also seeking SBay's further advice, because some of them may not be necessary in the current phase.

Issue to focus on
The full list is confirmed complete; what is not yet clear is the priority for this phase. We would like SBay to help review the committed items and advise which are genuinely required now (e.g. for the first US shipment and current sales channel) versus which can be deferred to a later phase — so NOWA can sequence testing and documentation correctly. Advice on whether any ◻ HQTS-suggested items are needed at this stage is also welcome.
How to read this
  • ✅ Committed — NOWA intends to do this (checked "to-do" on the Notion board), pending SBay's advice on whether it is needed in the current phase. ◻ HQTS-suggested — HQTS recommends but it is not committed.
  • Type: Mandatory (required by US law) · Contingent (required only if a condition applies) · Voluntary (recommended) · Confirming (still being decided).
  • Risk: High Risk Medium Risk Low Risk.
  • § under each requirement = the governing Law & Standard; Region shows where it applies. The detail row (below each item) explains what the requirement is, plus bilingual notes where available.
Categories explained
  • Test report (18) — Laboratory test reports or certificates proving the product (or a specific component) meets a safety or chemical standard — normally issued by an accredited lab.
  • Labeling & Packaging (10) — Marks, labels, logos and warnings that must physically appear on the product or its packaging (e.g. country of origin, FCC logo, age range, safety warnings).
  • Instructions (13) — Information to be provided in the user manual / instructions for use — operation, safety, maintenance, identifiers and similar.
  • Regulatory Affair (9) — Legal and regulatory obligations such as privacy rules, state registrations and chemical reporting — compliance with a law, rather than a physical test or label.

§ Illinois - Lead Poisoning Prevention Act
✅ Committed		 Test report
Plush Toy
Component: Enclosure, Silicone Skin, Attachment Band
US 🇺🇸
 Low Risk — Missing
What it is
A test report from an accredited laboratory verifying compliance with the Illinois Lead Poisoning Prevention Act: lead in all accessible parts must not exceed the limits (paint/coating < 90 ppm and base material < 100 ppm). Applies to articles or toys for children under 12 sold or distributed in Illinois.
To understand (ghi chú)
• Áp dụng khi nào?
Khi bạn bán hoặc phân phối sản phẩm tại tiểu bang Illinois, và sản phẩm là đồ dùng hoặc đồ chơi cho trẻ dưới 12 tuổi.
• Yêu cầu chính:
Test để đảm bảo hàm lượng chì trong vật liệu phủ hoặc sơn < 90 ppm, và vật liệu nền < 100 ppm.

• When does it apply?
When you sell or distribute a product in the state of Illinois, and the product is an article or toy for children under 12 years of age.
• Main requirement:
Test to ensure that the lead content in the coating or paint material is <90 ppm, and the base material is <100 ppm.
